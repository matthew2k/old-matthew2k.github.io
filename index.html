About Me

My name is Matthew Li, and I am a student at the Texas Academy of Math and Science , an accelerated program within the University of North Texas, studying economic public policy. I’m mainly interested in international economics, specifically the patterns and consequences of an increasingly globalized economy. As an economist, I hope to use public policy to improve relations between different countries, and strngthen the relationship between a government and its people to help less fortunate and raise the standard of living for everyone. Currently, I’m working with Dr. Michael Carroll of the UNT Economics Research Group. The focus of my research is on regional economic policy, and how that impacts the day to day operations of a city. Additionally, I write economic opinion pieces for the North Texas Daily, UNT’s student newspaper. When I’m not balancing budgets or elaborating the obvious, I can be found competing as an epeeist for the UNT fencing team.
